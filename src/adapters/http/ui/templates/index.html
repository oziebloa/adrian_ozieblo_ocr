OCR (Optical Character Recognition)

is a technology that allows computers to recognize and interpret printed or handwritten text from images,
scanned documents, or other sources. OCR software uses algorithms and machine learning techniques to analyze
the shapes, patterns, and other features of the characters in an image and convert them into digital text that can be edited,
searched, or stored in a database.

OCR is widely used in industries such as finance, healthcare, and government, where there is a need to process
large volumes of paper-based documents quickly and accurately. It can help automate tasks such as data entry,
invoice processing, and form filling, and improve efficiency and productivity in various business processes.
OCR is also used in document management systems, e-books, and other applications where text needs to be extracted from images.
